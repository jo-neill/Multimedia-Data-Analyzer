Flickr Filter Identification Findings and Results

1. Testing Parameters

Training and testing was performed on datasets containing three filters, five filters, and five filters plus a set of unfiltered images. All odd neighbor (k) values from 1-29 were evaluated to determine the value with optimal accuracy in each case. Various ratios of training to testing data were also experimented with. Optimal conditions were logged for each dataset tested.

3. Error Sources & Concessions

The most immediate source of error directly pertains to the training data available. Flickr does not indicate what filters have been applied to images automatically or after a user has enabled one, hence the opportunity to undertake this project. Filters must be attempted to be identified by eye and categorized as such. This proves a difficult task with more subtle or similar filters. This suspicion is substantiated by a high degree of accuracy when testing the most obvious and likely to be correctly identified filters in the three filter set.

An additional concession is inherently the color composition of the images in the datasets. While filters do heavily alter the HSV composition of images, darker or lighter images regardless of applied filter may already be more predisposed to being recognized as noir or bleached respectively, regardless of another filter being applied.
